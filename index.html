Mekelle

Visit Mekelle: The capital of Tigray.

The Best Historical Places to Visit in Mekelle

1. The Martyrs' Memorial Monument

The Martyrs' Memorial Monument pays respects to the over 60,000 TPLF fighters who died and over 100,000 fighters who were injured in the overthrow of the Marxist Derg regime in 1991.

The monument is 51 metres (167 ft) tall and is located in Adi-Haki sub-city. The monument has many sculptures depicting the struggle of the peasants and fighters against the Derg soldiers.

2. Abreha Castle (Enda Dejat Abraha)

A local lord built Abreha Castle, which has a very similar architectural design to Emperor Yohannes' palace, in the 1890s.

At present, the castle is a hotel, commanding a magnificent view of Mekelle.

3. Emperor Yohannes IV Castle

In the 19th century, Emperor Yohannes IV became Negus and built a castle and Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo churches in the city.

The castle was constructed by Ethiopian engineer Lij Ingidashet and Italian engineer Giacomo Naretti. The castle complex now serves as a museum where the Emperor's throne, royal bed, ceremonial dress, rifles, and many other valuable historical collections can be seen.
